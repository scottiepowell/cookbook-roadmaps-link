# ADR: Website Marketing and Community Outreach

Status: proposed, docs/research-only

Date: 2026-07-23

## Decision summary

Market the Cookbook AI product through useful, original, transparent content
and genuine community relationships. Treat the idea of using cooking-video
transcripts to generate comment drafts as a narrow exploration only, never as a
commenting system or growth automation. Any external comment must be selected,
reviewed, edited, and posted by a human who is genuinely participating in the
conversation. The project must reject bots, account automation, mass or
repetitive comments, fake engagement, impersonation, undisclosed promotion,
and platform-limit evasion.

Safer first experiments are owned recipe landing pages, cooking-problem blog
posts, creator outreach, guest content, newsletters with proper consent,
public build-in-public updates, and community participation that adds value
before promotion. No marketing automation, scraper, transcript downloader,
browser automation, API client, comment poster, or integration is approved by
this ADR.

## Research note and policy constraints

Public primary/reputable guidance was reviewed on 2026-07-23. It is not legal
advice and must be rechecked for the target country, platform, account, and
campaign:

- YouTube's [Spam, deceptive practices, and scams policy](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/2801973)
  applies to comments, links, posts, and other community features. It calls
  out high-volume repetitive/deceptive comments, artificial engagement,
  off-platform diversion, automated/synthetic mass production, and scraped
  content.
- YouTube's [impersonation policy](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/2801947)
  prohibits misleadingly pretending to be another person, entity, or channel,
  including through AI-generated likeness or identity signals.
- YouTube's [copyright guidance](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/2797466)
  explains that original videos are generally copyright-protected and that
  permission, a valid license, or a jurisdiction-appropriate exception must be
  evaluated; credit or a disclaimer alone is not permission. The [U.S.
  Copyright Office fair-use FAQ](https://www.copyright.gov/help/faq/faq-fairuse.html)
  notes that fair use is fact-specific and has no fixed safe word count.
- The [FTC's influencer disclosure guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/disclosures-101-social-media-influencers)
  says material connections such as payment, employment, or valuable perks
  should be disclosed clearly and conspicuously with the endorsement.

These sources do not create a universal permission to reuse transcripts or
promote in comments. Terms, copyright, privacy, spam, disclosure, and creator
expectations must be checked for each proposed activity.

## Marketing goals and audiences

Goals should be specific and user-benefiting:

- help people solve real cooking and recipe-organization problems;
- explain the value and limits of Cookbook AI clearly;
- attract users who want recipe import, search, planning, or portable data;
- build trust with home cooks, creators, educators, and potential partners;
- learn which content creates qualified, privacy-aware visits and workflow use.

Potential audiences include home cooks, people with scattered recipe notes,
meal planners, accessibility-minded cooks, recipe creators, and small creator
partners. The product should not target children or infer sensitive health or
dietary traits for marketing. Audience claims must be evidence-based and not
promise unsupported AI accuracy, medical outcomes, or guaranteed savings.

## Acceptable channels

Acceptable channels are those where the project can be authentic, transparent,
and manually accountable:

- SEO recipe pages and landing pages for specific cooking problems;
- original blog posts, comparisons, demos, and build-in-public changelogs;
- short-form clips or graphics made from owned/licensed material;
- Pinterest-style recipe graphics with rights-cleared assets and links;
- creator outreach by email with identity, purpose, and disclosure;
- guest posts and educational collaborations;
- an opt-in newsletter with clear sender identity and unsubscribe handling;
- affiliate or partner content only with written terms and clear disclosure;
- community participation without promotion-first behavior.

Every channel needs an owner, approval boundary, content rights record, and
privacy-aware measurement plan. Marketing must not be used to bypass product
quality, auth, data ownership, or provider safety gaps.

## YouTube transcript/comment idea: exploration only

The only potentially acceptable concept is:

- a human selects a small number of relevant videos/channels;
- transcript or source review is permitted by the source, license, terms, or a
  separately reviewed legal basis;
- an AI tool may privately help the site owner brainstorm a draft;
- no transcript or draft is committed to the repository or published
  automatically;
- a human reviews, edits, and decides whether to post;
- any comment is genuinely relevant to the video and adds useful cooking
  context rather than merely advertising;
- any promotion is transparent and non-deceptive;
- there are no repeated templates, mass campaigns, impersonation, bot
  accounts, fake engagement, or evasion of platform limits.

The default recommendation is not to use comments as a primary acquisition
channel. A comment that exists mainly to send viewers to Cookbook AI is
promotion-first and should be rejected. A thoughtful answer that would still
be worth posting if the website link were removed may be eligible for human
review, but a link or disclosure must remain truthful and appropriate to the
platform context.

## Prohibited practices

The project must not:

- automate posting, account creation, account rotation, likes, replies, or
  engagement;
- scrape videos, comments, channels, or transcripts without permission and
  terms/copyright review;
- download or store copyrighted transcripts as a marketing dataset;
- generate or publish large batches of similar comments;
- use fake identities, bot accounts, impersonation, or undisclosed AI-driven
  promotion;
- post irrelevant links, repeated calls to action, misleading claims, or
  comments designed only to divert traffic;
- buy engagement, coordinate artificial engagement, or evade rate limits and
  moderation systems;
- present a sponsor, affiliate, employment, or financial relationship as an
  independent recommendation;
- collect personal data from commenters or target people using sensitive
  inferred attributes.

Violations can harm creators, users, platform accounts, and the Cookbook
brand. A human click on “post” is not enough if the surrounding process is
mass-produced, deceptive, or unauthorized.

## Quality, review, and disclosure standard

Before any external outreach, a human reviewer must confirm:

1. the message is original, accurate, concise, and directly relevant;
2. it adds value independent of the promotion;
3. it does not impersonate the creator or imply endorsement;
4. claims about Cookbook AI are supported and appropriately qualified;
5. source material was obtained and used under permission, license, terms, or
   a documented legal review;
6. the platform's current rules allow the activity;
7. any material connection is disclosed clearly where the message appears;
8. the message is not repetitive or part of a mass campaign;
9. the reviewer records a safe decision, not the transcript or raw prompt.

If the site owner has a financial, employment, affiliate, free-service, or
other material connection, the endorsement must make that relationship obvious
in plain language. Disclosure cannot be hidden after a “more” link or buried
among hashtags. The exact wording needs jurisdiction and platform review.

## Transcript and copyright handling

Ideas, recipe techniques, and factual cooking concepts may be discussed, but a
transcript is a written expression that may be protected. Do not assume that
public availability, attribution, a short excerpt, AI transformation, or fair
use makes a commercial marketing workflow safe. For a future experiment:

- prefer creator-provided transcripts, permission, a clear license, or original
  notes from a permitted viewing;
- do not build a transcript corpus, downloader, scraper, or repository dataset;
- retain only a minimal rights/permission record and a short human summary if
  necessary for review;
- do not reproduce substantial transcript text in comments or marketing copy;
- get legal review for uncertain commercial use, derivative use, or recurring
  processing;
- honor takedown, opt-out, creator requests, and platform terms promptly.

## Metrics for future experiments

Use privacy-aware aggregate metrics only after the analytics ADR is implemented
and the purpose/consent boundary is approved:

```text
traffic source
landing-page views
click-through rate
bounce/exit rate
return visits
recipe import starts
AI workflow starts
session completions
newsletter signups
BYOS/SSO interest
ad/sponsor clicks when monetization exists
qualitative feedback
complaints/spam reports
```

Metrics should be interpreted with small samples and not optimized at the
expense of community trust. Complaints, spam reports, creator requests, and
platform warnings are stop-signal metrics, not merely negative conversion
data.

## Safe experiment design and go/no-go criteria

Any future outreach experiment requires:

- a small, pre-approved batch and a named human owner;
- manual review and editing of every external message;
- clear disclosure where a material connection exists;
- no automation, scraping, or transcript storage without permission/terms
  review;
- no generated comments committed to the repository;
- privacy-aware measurement only, with no raw content or personal profiles;
- explicit stop conditions for negative feedback, complaints, takedowns,
  platform warnings, copyright uncertainty, or misleading claims.

Go only when the content would be welcome without the promotion, rights and
platform rules are documented, disclosure is clear, the human reviewer can
explain the decision, and the experiment is small enough to stop safely. No-go
when the primary purpose is traffic diversion, the same content is reused at
scale, the source cannot be lawfully reviewed, automation is required, or the
relationship would be hidden from readers.

## Explicit non-goals

This ADR does not implement marketing automation, comment posting, transcript
scraping/download, browser automation, YouTube or other platform API clients,
account automation, marketing integrations, analytics, cookies, ad systems,
affiliate systems, payment, monetization, SSO/BYOS, production auth, public
routes, AWS/platform work, live provider calls, or content generation. No
transcripts, screenshots, traces, prompts, provider outputs, or marketing
comments are committed.
